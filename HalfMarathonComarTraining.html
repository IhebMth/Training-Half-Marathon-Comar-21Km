Race Strategy

Pre-Race (2-3 hours before)

  • Light breakfast 2-3 hours before (familiar foods)
  • Arrive at venue 90 minutes early
  • 15-minute warm-up: 2K easy + 4×100m strides + dynamic stretching

Pacing Strategy

  • Km 1-5: Start conservatively at 3:42-3:45/km (settle in)
  • Km 6-10: Hit target pace 3:39/km (find your rhythm)
  • Km 11-15: Maintain 3:39/km (stay strong)
  • Km 16-20: Push if feeling good, 3:35-3:39/km
  • Km 21-21.1: Everything you have left

Split Times

  • 5K: 18:30 (conservative start)
  • 10K: 36:25 (on target pace)
  • 15K: 54:40 (holding strong)
  • 20K: 1:12:55 (pushing phase)
  • Finish: 1:17:00 (GOAL!)

Nutrition & Hydration

Pre-Race

  • Carb load 2-3 days before race
  • Race morning: Banana, toast with honey, coffee (if usual)
  • Hydrate well but stop drinking 30 minutes before start

During Race

  • Small sips at water stations (every 5K)
  • Consider gel at 10K if needed (practice in training first)
  • Don't try anything new on race day

Training Tips & Notes

Weekly Structure

  • Sunday: Long runs (most important session)
  • Tuesday: Speed/Interval work
  • Thursday: Tempo/Threshold runs
  • Wednesday/Friday: Easy recovery runs
  • Saturday: Complete rest
  • Monday: Optional easy run or rest

Key Training Principles

  • Build volume gradually (10% rule)
  • Include recovery weeks every 4th week
  • Practice race pace in training regularly
  • Don't skip easy runs - they're crucial for adaptation
  • Listen to your body - adjust if needed

Monitoring Progress

  • Track weekly mileage and key workout paces
  • Monitor resting heart rate
  • Note how you feel during easy runs
  • Time trial every 4 weeks (5K or 10K)

Injury Prevention

  • Warm up before hard sessions
  • Cool down and stretch after all runs
  • Strength training 2x/week (core, hips, legs)
  • Replace shoes every 500-800K
  • Address any pain early

Final Notes

Flexibility: This plan is a guide. Adjust based on how you feel, weather, and life circumstances.

Recovery: Sleep 7-9 hours nightly. Recovery is when adaptation happens.

Consistency: It's better to complete 80% of sessions consistently than 100% sporadically.

Confidence: Trust your training. You have the fitness to achieve 1:17!

Good luck with your training and race day!
